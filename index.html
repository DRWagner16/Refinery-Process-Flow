Oil Refinery Process Overview

A conceptual overview of how crude oil is processed through major refinery units to produce usable fuels and products. Click a unit to learn more.

Distillation Tower

The distillation tower is the primary separation unit in a refinery. Crude oil is heated in a furnace and introduced into the column, where vapor–liquid equilibrium enables separation based on boiling point differences.

Products

  • Fuel gas and LPG
  • Naphtha
  • Kerosene / Jet Fuel
  • Atmospheric Gas Oil
  • Atmospheric Residue

Vacuum Distillation Unit

The vacuum distillation unit (VDU) processes atmospheric residue under reduced pressure (vacuum) to recover valuable heavy gas oils while minimizing thermal cracking.

Process Role

Extends crude utilization by separating heavy fractions that would otherwise be unsuitable for direct use.

Hydrotreating

Hydrotreating units chemically treat refinery streams with hydrogen over a catalyst to remove impurities like sulfur, nitrogen, and metals.

Key Reaction

R-S-R + 2H₂ → 2R-H + H₂S

Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC)

The FCC is the "workhorse" of the refinery. It breaks heavy gas oils into lighter, high-value products like gasoline and propylene using a fluidized catalyst.

Conceptual Operation

Preheated feed contacts hot catalyst in the riser. Carbon (coke) deposits on the catalyst during the reaction. The spent catalyst is sent to a regenerator where the coke is burned off, providing the heat for the process.

Hydrocracker

Hydrocrackers use high pressure hydrogen and catalyst to crack heavy feeds into diesel, jet fuel, and naphtha with very low sulfur content.

Delayed Coking

A severe thermal cracking process used on the heaviest residues. It "squeezes" the last liquid products out of the barrel, leaving behind solid petroleum coke.
